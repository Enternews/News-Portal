A World of Endless Possibilities: Invincible's Showrunners Reveal the Truth About Superhero Fatigue

ENTER News. It's time to face the music, folks! Allegedly from Polygon, the era of superhero domination may be behind us, but the showrunners of Invincible are here to tell us that's not entirely true. Robert Kirkman, the mastermind behind the hit animated series, believes that the issue isn't that fans are tired of superheroes, but rather that the genre needs to evolve to meet the changing tastes of the audience. And we couldn't agree more!

As Kirkman so astutely points out, "They were a very unique, exciting, new thing when Marvel movies started coming out two, three, four times a year." And we can't help but feel a sense of nostalgia for those early days when superhero movies were the epitome of excitement. But now, it seems that the audience has grown so accustomed to the storytelling that superhero stories need to elevate the genre. And that's exactly what Invincible does!

Simon Racioppa, Kirkman's co-showrunner, echoes his sentiments, emphasizing that simply being a good show isn't enough anymore. "You need to capture something unique, or smash in some other element that hasn't been there before, or just execute exceptionally well on all levels." And we couldn't agree more! When a show is as visually stunning and action-packed as Invincible, it's no wonder that fans are clamoring for more.

But what about the bigger picture? What about the fact that the Marvel Cinematic Universe has been dominating the box office for over a decade, with no signs of slowing down? Well, according to Kirkman, it's not just about superheroes anymore. "When you're on a streaming service, you're not competing with the 10 shows being produced right now, you're competing with every TV show that's ever been made." And that's a daunting task, indeed!
